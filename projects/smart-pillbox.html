Smart Pillbox for TB Patients in South Africa

Addressing Medication Non-Adherence Through Modular IoT Technology

Problem Statement

Medication non-adherence is a major challenge for tuberculosis (TB) patients in South Africa. TB treatment requires taking several pills every day at the right time and in the right amount, which many patients find difficult to manage. Long treatment periods, side effects, and having to keep track of multiple medications make the problem even worse. When patients miss doses or take their medication incorrectly, it can lead to treatment failure, drug resistance, ongoing spread of TB, and higher death rates. There is a strong need for solutions that help patients follow their medication schedules more easily and improve treatment outcomes.

How It Works

This smart pillbox follows a modular approach, designed to be flexible, affordable, and adaptable to diverse patient needs and circumstances across South Africa.

Optional Modules

Patients can customize their device by adding optional modules based on their specific needs and circumstances:

LCD Screen Module

Displays medication instructions, dosage information, and reminders visually. Ideal for patients who prefer reading instructions.

GSM Module

Enables real-time tracking via SMS. Healthcare professionals receive instant notifications when medication is taken or missed. Perfect for areas with cellular coverage.

Bluetooth Module

Connects to smartphones for app-based monitoring and reminders. Allows patients to track their adherence history on their mobile device.

Solar Module

Solar charging capability for rural areas with unreliable electricity. Ensures the device remains operational even without grid power.

WiFi Module

Internet connectivity for cloud-based data synchronization and remote monitoring. Suitable for urban areas with WiFi access.

Speaker Module

Audio instructions and reminders in preferred language. Essential for patients who struggle with reading or have visual impairments.

Development Process

1. Schematic Design

Designed the electronic schematic using KiCad software.

2. Breadboard Prototyping

Built initial prototype using a breadboard for testing circuit connections and validating the design before committing to Printed Circuit Board (PCB) fabrication.

3. ESP32 Programming

Programmed the ESP32 microcontroller using Arduino Integrated Development Environment (IDE), implementing medication reminder logic, sensor integration, and module communication protocols.

4. PCB Design

Designed custom printed circuit boards in KiCad, optimizing layout for compact size, reliable connections, and easy assembly of modular components.

5. Mechanical Design

Created 3D models and mechanical enclosure using Autodesk Inventor, ensuring proper fit for electronics, user-friendly access to medication, and modular port placement.

6. Assembly & Integration

Assembled the PCB components, integrated the circuit into the custom-designed casing, and tested all hardware functionality including sensors and module connections.

7. Android Application Development

Developed companion Android application using Android Studio and Java, providing user interface for medication tracking, reminders management, and adherence history visualization.

8. Database Design

Designed and implemented the database using Google Sheets with JavaScript for data management, enabling easy access for healthcare professionals and cost-effective cloud storage.

3D Design & Visualization

The mechanical design was created in Autodesk Inventor, featuring a modular architecture with expansion ports for optional modules. The design prioritizes ease of use, durability, and accessibility for TB patients.

Design Note

While TB patients typically require multiple medications daily, some cases involve single daily doses. This prototype was developed with one compartment to validate the feasibility of a modular architecture. In practice, additional compartments can be seamlessly integrated as needed. The primary objective was to demonstrate that a scalable, modular design approach is both practical and effective for medication dispensing systems.
